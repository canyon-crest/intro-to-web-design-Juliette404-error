Who am I? (A General Overview)

I am 18 years old, Korean American, and a high school senior in Canyon Crest Academy. I’m also the older sibling in my family, and we have a dog named Bobo who is now 11 years old. My favorite ice cream flavor is anything cookie-based (e.g. chocolate chip cookie dough, Blue Monster if at Handel’s, cookies & cream, etc.), and van Leeuwen’s and Handel’s are the best ice cream stores. One unique thing about me is that smell triggers my memory the most: I especially have deja-vu feelings in certain locations, like how a smoky air along the beach sidewalk reminds me of one alleyway in Seoul. Speaking of Seoul, I love to spend time with my relatives there as well as eat street food there, especially their mozzarella cheese corn dogs.

Interests & Hobbies

One of my main hobbies is playing my clarinet. I have been playing it since 2nd grade, and I really enjoy playing many different pieces with my ensembles from Marquez’s Danzon No. 2 to Shostakovich’s Symphony No. 4. I also love to go outside, mostly in nature (e.g. hiking in trails, seeing sunsets at a beach, camping with my family, etc.) because I find immersing myself in nature relaxing. Finally, I love to write from diary entries to poetry because I love to experiment on how I can express my ideas and feelings, as well as do photography because I believe in cherishing every moment in life. For my interests, I love to read Gothic Literature (my two favorites from that genre are Shelley’s Frankenstein and Brontë’s Jane Eyre), play card/board/video games with my family (my sister and I get pretty competitive against each other), discuss philosophical topics, and listen to a variety of music (depending on my mood).

What Music Feels to Me (As a Listener)

When I say that my favorite genre of music or song depends on my mood, it really does. Oftentimes, it depends on the weather, especially when I walk to school/home: I would play my “Sunny Days” playlist for sunny days and “Cloudy Days” for … you can guess. Whenever I study, I would listen to lofi hip-hop or Korean indie songs; however, when I study for a test, I would listen to powerful film scores or rock songs in the 80s from Aerosmith to Metallica to really concentrate. The strangest thing about listening to music is how certain songs remind me of certain eras in my life, like when I listen to Chappell Roan or ABBA, I remember my junior year. It’s a really cool thing to feel so much when I listen to music, no matter what I’m doing or where I am at that moment.
